Pricing & Quotes

How much does artificial turf installation cost in Windsor, Ontario?
Most residential turf projects in Ontario range from $8 to $18 per square foot installed, depending on turf grade and the complexity of site preparation. Final pricing depends on your specific yard size, surface type, and the product selected. We provide free written estimates with no obligation — contact us to get a number specific to your project.
Does artificial grass hold up in Ontario winters with snow and freezing temperatures?
Yes — modern artificial turf is engineered for Canadian climate conditions. The fibres, backing, and infill are all rated for freeze-thaw cycles. You can leave snow on the turf and it won't damage the surface. Most Windsor homeowners are pleasantly surprised by how well it looks coming out of winter.
Does synthetic turf get too hot in summer?
Artificial turf can absorb heat on very sunny days, though shaded areas and pool-adjacent installations tend to stay cooler. For areas where heat is a concern — like a children's play space — we can discuss infill options that reduce surface temperature. A quick rinse with the hose cools the surface down in minutes.
How long does artificial grass last in Canada?
Quality artificial turf installed over a proper base typically lasts 15 to 20 years in Canadian conditions. Lifespan depends on turf grade, the amount of traffic, and how the surface is maintained. Keeping it clean and brushing the fibres periodically extends its life significantly.

Installation Process

Is artificial turf safe for kids and pets?
The turf products we install are non-toxic, lead-free, and free of harmful chemicals. For pet areas, we recommend specific infill products that control odour and support good drainage. For play areas, we select turf with appropriate cushioning for falls.
Do I need to remove my natural lawn before installing artificial grass?
No — that's part of our job. We handle the removal and disposal of existing grass, soil, and any debris as part of the installation process. You don't need to prepare the yard before we arrive.
How does artificial turf compare to real grass for long-term maintenance costs in Ontario?
Over a 10-year period, most Ontario homeowners spend significantly more maintaining a natural lawn — water bills, fertilizer, overseeding, mowing equipment, and seasonal repairs add up quickly. Artificial turf eliminates nearly all of those ongoing costs. The upfront investment is higher, but the long-term economics tend to favour turf for most residential properties.
How long does installation take for a typical Windsor backyard?
Most standard residential backyards in Windsor complete in one to two days. Larger projects or those with complex site conditions may require an additional day. We'll give you a realistic timeline at the free estimate stage.

Materials & Quality

Do I need a permit to install artificial turf in Windsor?
In most cases, residential artificial turf installation in Windsor does not require a building permit. However, requirements can vary depending on the scope of the project — particularly if grading or drainage work is involved. We'll let you know if any permits are required when we assess your property.
Do you offer a warranty on your artificial turf installations?
Yes. Every Windsor Turf Pros installation includes a workmanship warranty. If there's a defect in how the turf was installed — edges lifting, seams opening, drainage issues — we'll come back and fix it. Product warranty terms vary by turf grade and will be outlined in your written quote.
What sub-base do you use under artificial turf?
We use a compacted crushed stone sub-base as the standard foundation for all our installations. This provides the drainage and structural stability that makes the turf sit level and drain correctly over years of use. Some elevated or specialty installations use alternative bases, which we'll explain at the estimate stage.
Can artificial turf be installed on a sloped yard?
Yes — in fact, artificial turf often performs better than natural grass on slopes because it doesn't wash out or erode. We assess slope grade during the site visit and adjust the base preparation and drainage plan accordingly.

Service Areas

What infill do you use, and why does it matter?
Infill is the material brushed into the turf fibres to keep them upright and add cushioning. The type we recommend depends on the application — standard silica sand for general use, zeolite for pet areas, and shock-pad systems for play zones. We'll recommend the right infill for your specific project.
